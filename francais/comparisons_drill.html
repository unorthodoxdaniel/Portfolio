Degree & Comparison Drill — Noble Method

The three levers...

French comparisons use just three words to do almost everything: plus (more), moins (less), and aussi (as). Master the feel of those three, and the superlative, intensity, and quantity structures all fall into place.

The key pattern to spot: plus/moins/aussi… que — the adjective or adverb lives between them, and que always introduces what you're comparing to. For quantities, plus de / moins de replaces the adjective with a noun.
