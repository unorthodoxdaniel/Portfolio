The sandwich rule...

French negation works like a sandwich: ne goes before the verb, and the second word (pas, plus, jamais...) goes after it. The verb is always the filling.

In spoken French, the ne often disappears entirely — "Je sais pas" is what you'll actually hear. But you need both halves to write and read properly.

The negation words highlighted in gold are the ones doing the work. Learn to spot them and the sentence opens up.
